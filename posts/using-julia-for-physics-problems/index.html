Using Julia for Physics Problems

Not only is Julia incredibly fast, easy to use, and multi-purpose in the conventional sense, but by using either a Jupyter Notebook, Pluto Notebook, or the REPL, it is an incredible tool for real-time computations. Here are some examples of using Julia to perform real-time calculations and solve physics problems!

Two students, \(S_1\) and \(S_2\), are rushing to class. They are waiting for a green light at the same intersection. As soon as the light turns green, both students start walking with constant acceleration. The acceleration of student \(S_1\) is \(a_1\), and the acceleration of student \(S_2\) is \(a_2\), When student \(S_1\) has reached a velocity \(v_1\), she has walked a distance \(d_1\), while student \(S_2\) has walked a distance \(d_2 = \frac{1}{3}d_1\). In terms of \(v_1\) and \(d_1\), what is the correct expression for \(a_2\), the acceleration of student \(S_2\)?
