Planar Orthogonality

Suppose we have two lines, y1y_1y1​ and y2y_2y2​, defined by,

y1≡m1x1+b1  and, y_1 \equiv m_1 x_1 + b_1\,\text{ and,} y1​≡m1​x1​+b1​ and, y2≡m2x2+b2 y_2 \equiv m_2 x_2 + b_2y2​≡m2​x2​+b2​

If the two lines are orthogonal, then,

m1⋅m2=−1 m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1m1​⋅m2​=−1
