Fermat's Little Theorem

Fermat's Little Theorem states that if ppp is a prime number, then for any integer aaa, the number ap−aa^p - aap−a is an integer multiple of ppp,

ap≡a(modp) . a^p \equiv a \pmod{p}\,.ap≡a(modp).

When gcd⁡(a,p)=1\gcd(a, p) = 1gcd(a,p)=1, then,

ap−1≡1(modp) . a^{p-1} \equiv 1 \pmod{p} \,.ap−1≡1(modp).
