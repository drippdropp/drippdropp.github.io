Finding a Trend Line with Least Squares

Suppose we have a bunch of data points in y\mathbf{y}y. We create a design matrix, XXX which consists of the pattern that we want to match. The first column will be all 111's because that's the constant term. The second column will be the numbers 111 through NNN for the linear term. If we wanted a quadratic term, we could include an x2x^2x2 sequence as a third column or for a cubic term a x3x^3x3 sequence as a fourth column.

X=[111213⋮⋮1N]X = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 3 \\ \vdots & \vdots \\ 1 & N \end{bmatrix}X=⎣⎡​111⋮1​123⋮N​⎦⎤​

To determine the trendline, we need to solve the equation,
