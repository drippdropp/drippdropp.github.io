A Quick Check for Divisibility by Eleven

There's a quick trick for multiplying any two digit number by \(11\).

Suppose you have a base ten number,

\[ d_1 \cdot 10 + d_0 \cdot 1\,,\]

then when multiplying by eleven the result will be,

\[ \begin{cases}d_1 \cdot 100 + (d_1 + d_0) \cdot 10 + d_0 \cdot 1 & \text{ if }d_1 + d_0 \leq 9 \\ (1 + d_1) \cdot 100 + (d_1 + d_0)\mod{10} \cdot 10 + d_0 \cdot 1 & \text{ if }d_1 + d_0 > 9\\ \end{cases}\]

For example, if we wanted to multiple \(11\) by \(43\), the solution would be \(473\) or the expansion would be,

\[ 4\cdot 10 + 3\cdot 1 \rightarrow 4\cdot 100 + (4 + 3)\cdot 10 + 3 \cdot 1\,.\]

Or if we wanted to multiply \(11\) by \(75\), the result would be \(825\) because \(7+5 = 12 > 9\), so we bump the \(7\) to \(8\) and then we have \(825\).
