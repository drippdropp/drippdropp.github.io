Problem 10

If f′′(x)=f′(x)f^{\prime\prime}(x) = f^\prime(x)f′′(x)=f′(x) for all real xxx and if f(0)=0f(0) = 0f(0)=0 and f′(0)=−1f^\prime(0) = -1f′(0)=−1, then f(x)f(x)f(x) is what?

This is one of those GRE questions which are solved by process of elimination.

The options are:

  • (a) 1−ex1-e^x1−ex

  • (b) ex−1e^x - 1ex−1

  • (c) e−x−1e^{-x} - 1e−x−1

  • (d) e−xe^{-x}e−x

  • (e) −ex-e^x−ex

Since we know that e0=1e^0 = 1e0=1 and f(0)=0f(0) = 0f(0)=0, that immediately means (d) and (e) are not possible. Since f′(x)=−1f^\prime(x) = -1f′(x)=−1, then (b) is not possible. Therefore it must be (a).
